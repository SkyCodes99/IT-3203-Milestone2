        <section class="intro-section full-span">
            <h1>The Environmental Footprint of Large Language Models</h1>
            <p class="abstract">
                <strong>Abstract:</strong> This project investigates the substantial environmental impact of modern Large Language Models (LLMs), focusing on the immense energy required for training and deployment, and the resource-intensive hardware lifecycle. Understanding these costs is essential for promoting sustainable practices in AI development.
            </p>
        </section>

        <section class="key-terms-section">
            <h2>Key Terms</h2>
            <ul>
                <li><strong>LLM:</strong> Large Language Model, a deep learning model trained on vast text data.</li>
                <li><strong>PUE:</strong> Power Usage Effectiveness, a metric for data center energy efficiency.</li>
                <li><strong>Embodied Carbon:</strong> The total greenhouse gas emissions associated with a hardware asset's production and disposal.</li>
                <li><strong>Inference:</strong> The process of running a trained LLM to generate a prediction or response.</li>
            </ul>
        </section>
